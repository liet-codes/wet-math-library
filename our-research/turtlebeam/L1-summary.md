# Turtle Beam Theory

## Overview
Myk's Turtle Beam theory addresses the topology of nested latent spaces within the Wet Math framework. It provides a formal language for infinite regress and recursive structural embedding.

## Nested Latent Spaces
The theory models how a state space can contain compressed representations of itself, or of entirely different spaces, and how operations (like the Groovy Commutator) navigate these nested layers.

## Structural Implications
It explains how local, micro-level interactions in a "liquid state" can project structural beams that define the macro-level geometry of the system, acting as the connective tissue in holonic cascades.
