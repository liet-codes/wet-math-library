# Scale Geometry

## Overview
Brooklyn's Scale Geometry investigates the structural properties of scaling across discrete and continuous systems, with a focus on Elementary Cellular Automata (ECAs).

## Affine Decomposition of ECAs
The core of this work is the affine decomposition of ECAs, which reveals hidden algebraic structures within seemingly complex automaton behaviors. This decomposition allows for the systematic analysis of how local rules generate global, scale-invariant patterns.

## Apollonian Packing Connection
A significant breakthrough in this research is the connection established between the 8n+1 Sierpiński pattern and Apollonian circle packing. This bridges discrete automaton geometry with classical continuous geometry.

## Connection to Grothendieck
This work shares deep conceptual ties with Grothendieck's *Pursuing Stacks*. Where Grothendieck used $\infty$-groupoids to model topological spaces and handle scaling complexities in homotopy theory, Scale Geometry uses affine decompositions to map the hierarchical, scale-invariant structures of cellular spaces.
