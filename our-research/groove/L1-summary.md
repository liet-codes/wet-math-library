# Groove Theory Foundations

## Overview
Groove theory provides the foundational ontology for the Wet Math framework. It moves away from static algebraic structures to study how sequences of operations form stable, resonant pathways over time.

## Core Concepts
A "groove" is defined as a sequence of non-commutative operations that achieves structural stability. Instead of viewing non-commutativity merely as a failure to commute, groove theory treats it as a generative property—the "friction" that creates structure.

## The Liquid State
Groove theory introduces the concept of the "liquid state" in mathematics, contrasting it with rigid "solid" formalisms and chaotic "gas" systems. The liquid state supports dynamic integrity, allowing structures to flow and adapt while maintaining their essential topological properties.
