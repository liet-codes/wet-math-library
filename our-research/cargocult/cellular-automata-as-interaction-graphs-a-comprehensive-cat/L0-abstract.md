# L0 Abstract

This paper unifies diverse cellular automata types under an "interaction graph" framework, systematically cataloging their structures, identifying shared rule spaces, algebraically characterizing rule embeddings, and defining tractability boundaries for exhaustive analysis.

By specializing generalized Cayley graphs to periodic, uniform-in-degree interaction graphs, the authors provide a comprehensive catalog of standard CA types (elementary, block, multi-dimensional lattices, Archimedean tilings) as specific graph isomorphisms. Their enumeration reveals that named types constitute a small, highly symmetric subset of a much larger space of possible interaction graphs, particularly for one-dimensional configurations. A detailed algebraic analysis of block CA embedding demonstrates that different interaction graph topologies can constrain the accessible algebraic structures, creating disjoint nonlinear rule subspaces even within a shared rule space. The work culminates in precise tractability boundaries, enabling exhaustive classification of binary CAs up to n=5 and 3-color ECAs with modern computational resources, offering a concrete research program for exploring the interplay between algebraic rule structure and causal topology.
