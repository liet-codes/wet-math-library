**Title:** Before Prediction: The Mathematics Was Right. The Ontology Was Optional.

**Author:** Working draft — March 2026. The author is not explicitly named in the provided text, which appears to be a conceptual paper or manifesto.

**Main Thesis:**
This paper asserts that Karl Friston's Variational Free Energy Principle (FEP) offers a profoundly correct and broadly applicable mathematical framework for understanding the dynamics of persistent coupled systems. However, it critically argues that the dominant *computational interpretation* of FEP—which characterizes the brain as a "prediction engine" performing "inference" through "generative models"—is an optional and often misleading conceptual layer. Instead, the paper proposes that the FEP's mathematics fundamentally describes the physical processes of "coupling formation, maintenance, and renegotiation" within dynamical systems. These processes are posited to be driven by mechanisms such as synchrony-rewarding plasticity at the synapse, temporal compression of patterns into low-dimensional manifolds at the network level, and the sustained maintenance of long-range order through timescale separation at the systems level. The overarching argument is that biological intelligence operates along a continuum from basic coupled dynamics to formal computation, with the brain primarily existing in a "rich middle" where its continuous, noisy, and context-sensitive dynamics enable flexible, adaptive behavior without necessarily satisfying the strict criteria of formal computation.

It then introduces the predictive processing framework, acknowledging the mathematical brilliance of FEP but highlighting a "subtle mismatch" with its universal computational interpretation, referencing work by Wolpert and Korbel (2026) to distinguish imposed computational descriptions from discovered mechanisms.

It connects these physical substrates to the mathematics of inference via reservoir computing, showing how homeostatic dynamics can mimic predictive processing without implementing inference. The section concludes by re-conceptualizing memory as "curvature in the coupling substrate."
3.  **Coupling Renegotiation Across Scales:** The paper then broadens its scope, presenting a range of examples from various biological scales (slime molds, ant colonies, predator species, fish schools, starling murmurations) to illustrate how the concept of coupling renegotiation provides explanatory power without recourse to computational vocabulary. 4.  **Empirical Stories Distinguishing Frameworks:** Two key empirical narratives are presented to directly differentiate the coupling framework from computational accounts:
    *   **Anesthesia:** Studies (2024, 2025) showing anesthetics disrupt cortical-subcortical timescale separation and reduce individual brain distinguishability, while ketamine dissociates external from internal coupling, supporting the idea of persistent internal coherence.
    *   **The Mastery Transition:** Longitudinal piano training studies and meta-analyses of inter-brain synchrony in teamwork (2024) reveal that mastery correlates with *reduced* active coupling and processing, contrary to computational predictions, and consistent with coupling becoming self-sustaining. 5.  **What Computation Actually Is:** This section addresses the ambiguity of "computation," providing a rigorous definition based on Gualtiero Piccinini's mechanistic account (medium-independent, norm-governed manipulation). It argues that digital computers achieve this through engineering ("digital abstraction"), whereas the brain's continuous, noisy, and medium-dependent dynamics generally do not. A "continuum" from coupled dynamics to formal computation is proposed, with the brain operating in the "rich middle."
6.  **The Expansion (Conclusion):** The paper concludes by reiterating FEP's broad applicability to all persistent coupled systems, distinguishing it from its limited computational interpretation. It synthesizes the proposed physical processes (synchrony, temporal compression, timescale separation) and their implications for understanding memory, mastery, and the organism-environment relationship. Finally, it outlines missing pieces for future research, particularly the full development of the continuum of intelligence and the question of what intelligence is beyond computation.

**Key Claims:**
*   Synaptic plasticity (STDP) at biologically relevant timescales rewards synchrony (co-firing) rather than causal order, fundamentally shaping brain architecture around coherence.
*   Friston's Free Energy Principle is a mathematically sound description of persistent coupled systems, but its computational interpretation (e.g., generative models, prediction error, inference) is an optional conceptual layer that often misrepresents the actual physical mechanisms.
*   The FEP's mathematics actually describes physical processes: synchrony-rewarding plasticity, temporal compression into low-dimensional manifolds, and the maintenance of long-range order via timescale separation, which mechanistically re-frames the phenomenon of criticality.
*   Memory is reconceptualized not as stored models, but as "curvature in the coupling substrate" that biases future dynamics without explicit representation.
*   Biological intelligence manifests through diverse coupling renegotiation mechanisms across all scales (from slime molds to social groups), often without requiring a computational explanation.
*   Anesthesia disrupts cortical-subcortical timescale separation, degrading coherent coupling, while ketamine uniquely shows internal coherence can persist independently of external coupling.
*   The "mastery transition" in skill acquisition and teamwork involves a shift from active coupling renegotiation to self-sustaining coupling, characterized by *reduced* active processing, contrary to computational expectations.
*   "Computation," when rigorously defined (e.g., via Piccinini), represents an idealized regime. Brain dynamics, being continuous, noisy, and context-sensitive, typically operate in a "rich middle" on a continuum from coupled dynamics to formal computation.
*   The organism-environment relationship is co-dynamical and shaped by fitness-relevant salience (as in a "softer" reading of Donald Hoffman's interface theory), rather than solely veridical representation.
