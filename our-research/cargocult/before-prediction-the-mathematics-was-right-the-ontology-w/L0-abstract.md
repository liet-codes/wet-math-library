**2. L0 Abstract**

This paper argues that while the mathematics of predictive processing is universally applicable to persistent coupled systems, its common computational interpretation of brain function is an optional ontology that obscures the underlying physical processes of coupling renegotiation driven by synchrony and timescale dynamics.

By re-examining empirical findings from synaptic plasticity, network dynamics, systems neuroscience, and the effects of anesthesia and mastery, this paper challenges the prevailing computational metaphor for the brain. It posits that Friston's Free Energy Principle, while mathematically robust, primarily describes the formation, maintenance, and renegotiation of coherent coupling between dynamical systems, rather than explicit prediction or inference. The proposed framework explains phenomena like memory as "curvature in the coupling substrate" and intelligence as operating along a continuum of coupled dynamics, arguing that brain function often resides in a "rich middle" where continuous, noisy, and context-sensitive physical dynamics support adaptive behavior without adhering to the strict criteria of formal computation. This perspective highlights the need to understand biological intelligence as something broader than computation, emphasizing the co-dynamical relationship between organisms and their environments.
