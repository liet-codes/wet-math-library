---

The paper introduces "Shannon clusters" as a unifying framework to describe the recurring structural problem of identifying stable dynamical regimes, their informational boundaries, and robust descriptions under coupling, found across renormalization group theory, open quantum systems, quantum error correction, and dynamical systems. This framework provides a common vocabulary to highlight structural parallels between diverse formalisms that independently address this problem, such as RG fixed points, almost-invariant sets, pointer states, and holographic encoding. By reframing these as domain-specific instances of retained informational regimes, the paper offers methodological value for cross-domain transfer, new research directions in information geometry and intra-cluster dynamics, and a reinterpretation of concepts like the observer paradox and the regime-specificity of physical laws, without claiming formal identity or deriving new physical results.
