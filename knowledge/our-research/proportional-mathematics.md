# Proportional Mathematics

## Overview
Patrick's Proportional Mathematics is a fundamental reframing of arithmetic and algebra, prioritizing relationships and ratios over absolute values.

## Vessels and Fill-States
The core ontology replaces static numbers with "vessels" and "fill-states". A value is understood not in isolation, but by how it fills a structural vessel. This approach naturally handles dynamic transformations and scaling.

## Construction History (+1 as trace)
A critical innovation is treating operations like "+1" not just as quantitative additions, but as structural traces—a record of the construction history of the state. This memory-retentive math ensures that how a state was reached remains part of its identity.

## The 8n+1 Sierpiński Connection
The framework naturally generates the 8n+1 Sierpiński structure, which links directly to $\sigma_3$ / Eisenstein series, grounding the proportional approach in deep number theory.

The Crossover at e

As you slide n, the ordering of the nine values changes. Some powers swap rank. Different pairs commute (go orange) at different values of n — for instance, 2^4 = 4^2 exactly at n=3, and 2.3^3.3 ≈ 3.3^2.3 near n=3.3.

The crossover point for a^b = b^a is governed by x^(1/x), which peaks at x = e. This peak is why e keeps appearing: it's where the function that determines whether two numbers can commute under exponentiation reaches its maximum. The number e is the symmetry point of exponentiation — not because the table is most orange there, but because it's the fulcrum around which all commutative pairs organize.

Key observations:

At n=3: 2^4 = 4^2 = 16 — exact commutation! The only integer solution to x^y = y^x (besides x=y).

At n≈3.3: 2.3^3.3 ≈ 3.3^2.3 — the (a,b) pair nearly commutes.

At n=e: no single pair is especially close, but e is where x^(1/x) peaks — the theoretical maximum of "commutability." All pairs that DO commute at other n values owe their existence to this peak.

As n grows larger, ALL pairs diverge further — exponentiation becomes increasingly non-commutative at scale.
