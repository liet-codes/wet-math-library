## our-research
- [[groovy-commutator]] — A formalization of non-commutative operations using D, I, and C operators.  The Groovy Commutator measures where the order of operations matters and whether that mattering has structure. It establishes a framework using Drop (D), Insert (I), and Commute (C) operators, defining D-depth levels to categorize structural complexity. This maps directly to Grothendieck's dessins d'enfants in Esquisse as a way of exploring non-commutative geometries through discrete operational sequences.
- [[groove-theory]] — An introduction to Groove theory as the foundation of Wet Math.  Groove theory establishes the conceptual groundwork for understanding structural resonance and patterns in mathematical systems. It defines how non-commutative sequences create stable, self-reinforcing pathways or "grooves," linking abstract algebra to dynamic system behavior.
- [[holonic-metabolism]] — A framework for understanding mathematical systems through the lens of metabolic processes.  Holonic Metabolism applies biological concepts to mathematical structures, modeling them as dynamic systems undergoing continuous anabolic (building) and catabolic (breaking down) cascades to maintain structural integrity.
- [[scale-geometry]] — A geometric approach to scaling, cellular automata, and Apollonian packing.  Scale Geometry explores the affine decomposition of Elementary Cellular Automata (ECAs) and connects these discrete systems to continuous concepts like Apollonian circle packing. It provides a structural link between scale-invariant phenomena and the LOGOS framework.
- [[self-ratio]] — An investigation into the fundamental nature of the d/d self-ratio.  The Self-Ratio Discovery centers on Patrick's power spectrum tables, analyzing the crossover at $e$ and the structural implications of a value divided by itself across different proportional scales.
- [[shannons-gap]] — A study of information retention and gaps in dynamic systems.  Shannon's Gap explores the boundaries of informational regimes, analyzing how systems retain, lose, or transform information across structural thresholds.
- [[proportional-mathematics]] — A foundational framework prioritizing proportional relationships over absolute quantities.  Proportional Mathematics redefines mathematical operations through vessels, fill-states, and construction histories (like +1 as trace). It emphasizes how values relate to one another dynamically rather than their static numerical identities.
- [[mcguffin]] — A tensor-based system for tracking proportional relationships.  The McGuffin Tensor System provides a multidimensional framework for mapping the vessels, fill-states, and structural transformations defined in Proportional Mathematics.
- [[turtle-beam]] — An exploration of nested latent spaces in mathematical models.  Turtle Beam theory examines the infinite regress and structural nesting of latent spaces, addressing how local interactions cascade into global structures ("turtles all the way down").
- [[bourbaki-connection]] — A synthesis connecting Wet Math to classical 20th-century mathematics.  The Bourbaki Connection situates the Wet Math framework within the historical context of Bourbaki and Grothendieck, exploring the "remainder of the remainder" as the frontier of liquid state mathematics.
